About Me

After graduating with first class honours from the University of York, UK in 2016 with a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics, I obtained a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics from Imperial College London, UK at the end of 2017. Since November of 2017 I am now a PhD student of Professor Nikolaus A. Adams at the Chair of Aerodynamics and Fluid Mechanics of the Technical University of Munich, where I work on the Uncertainty Quantification of Turbulent Reactive Flows.

Initially fascinated by the beauty and expressiveness of Stochastic Analysis, I decided to write my Bachelor Thesis under the supervision of Professor Zdzislaw Brzezniak and focus on Stochastic Differential Equations. Coming to Imperial my focus began to tilt more towards Fluid Dynamics, Numerical Analysis and the use of Stochastics in the two fields. Combining Fluid Dynamics with Numerical Analysis then lead me to write my Master Thesis on "Non-Nested Geometric Multigrid on Complex Domains" with Dr. Lawrence Mitchell as part of the Firedrake Project. My PhD topic now allows me to combine Fluid Dynamics, Numerical Analyis and Stochastics to analyse Turbulence Simulations of Reactive Shock-Bubble Interactions for their uncertainties.

More specifically I use Bayesian Statistics to analyse a planar shock wave hitting a reactive gas bubble (Reactive Shock-Bubble Interaction) which is highly sensitive to variations in the impacting-shock mach number, initial concentration and the initial shape of the bubble, while it being very expensive to compute a single numerical sample. As the true initial conditions (priors) are not known the problem has to be cast as an inverse problem, where we compute many samples of the simulation to improve the posterior to then subsequently improve the quality of the prior. Given the associated computational costs the current research focus is on a reduction of the costs to allow for a larger number of samples and a hence more accurate quantification of the uncertainties.

I am currently investigating multiple non-excluding avenues to reduce the computational costs such as Physics motivated model simplifications, the use of Active Subspaces to separate the chemical reaction and the Fluid Dynamics, the construction of Physics observant surrogate models and the use of low-rank Tensor decompositions. Another avenue of research I am currently pursuing is the use of Probabilistic Programming with Pyro to get a gauge on the sensitivity of the considered system and allow for the construction of a more informed prior. For more details on my current work please have a look at the Projects section.
